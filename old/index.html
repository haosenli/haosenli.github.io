Custom Mechanical Keyboards

This custom keyboards hobby is strangely addicting. One moment I'm just buying some switches to mod my keyboard with, and next thing I know, I got enough switches to fit in more than 10 keyboards.

It's impractical, it's expensive, it's time-consuming, and people will think you're insane for paying such prices for just a keyboard. However, finding that perfect combination of keyboard case design, keyboard plate material, switches, keycaps, and lubricants in order to generate the most pleasing aural and visual stimulation specifically tailored for you is just oh-so-satisfying.

But... I won't deny it, I've spent way too much money in this hobby.

Currently Listening

I am an avid classical music listener as well as a beginner pianist. I'm primarily into the classical piano repertoires (and sometimes violin).

My current listening obsession is Rachmaninov's Piano Concerto No.3. This particular concerto captured my interest after listening to Yunchan Lim's gold-medal winning performance of it in the 2022 Van Cliburn Piano Competition. Hearing Yuncham's historical performance of this composition once again made me realize how beautiful and triumphant this concerto truly is. I have been on a months-long journey to find my favorite Rachmaninov Piano Concerto No.3 performance/recording (they're all so good!). Besides Rachmaninov, I'm also delving deeper into Beethoven's piano works. In particular, his Piano Concerto No.3 & No.5.

Favorite Contemporary Pianists

  • Lang Lang
    My piano hero, he is a breathtaking pianist who has spectacular technical abilities as well as musical understanding. He is the reason why I got into classical piano music.
  • Yuja Wang
    She has phenomenal musical sensitivity as well as rhythmicity , I highly recommend her recording of the Rachmaninov Piano Concerto No.2 with the Mahler Chamber Orchestra. It is simply out of this world.
  • Daniil Trifonov
    If I had to describe his piano playing in one word, it would be transcendental. Daniil's performances are such a joy to listen to and watch. He has a recital at UW Meany Center on Nov. 8!
